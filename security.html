Security Council

The UN Security Council is one of the six main organs of the United Nations, with the primary responsibility of maintaining international peace and security. The Security Council takes the lead in determining the existence of threats to the peace or acts of aggression. It is composed of different members, five of which are permanent with veto power: China, France, Russia, the United Kingdom, and the United States.

The remaining members are non-permanent and elected by the General Assembly for two-year terms. It calls upon parties to disputes to settle them peacefully and can recommend methods of adjustment or terms of settlement.

● Confiscation of nuclear weapons.
● Limiting defence of powerful countries to “even the playing field.”
